Gorms

​Musician, DJ, and life of the party, Matt Gormley “DJ Gorms” hails from Philadelphia Pennsylvania. Outside of the DJ booth you’ll find him spending hours searching for a new sound to bring to the party. Although only 23, Gorms has developed a wide array of performing experiences. Recent graduate of Penn State, Gorms is well known for his residencies at Indigo Nightclub (Recently renamed, The Basement Nightspot), Chrome Liquor Lounge, and Champs Downtown. After his first Summer in Philadelphia, Gorms is quickly growing his name with appearances at JJ Bootleggers, Midtown Bar, and Fieldhouse in Philadelphia. In addition to his nightlife presence he has become a fan favorite of In the Mix special events. “I want to give them what they want, even if they don’t know what it is yet.” Gorms prides himself on a wide array of music that will get you moving

Apex

David a.k.a. DJ Apex, knew from a young age that he wanted to be the person to bring energy and music to the people. By any means necessary, David always found a way to be the person with music and speakers at any occasion to ensure all in attendance would have a great time. From carrying crates filled with vinyl records for one of the area’s most known and inspirational DJ’s, DJ Excel, to learning the ins and outs of sound from his Uncle, DJ Rab – Sound engineer for a local radio station; David learned the tricks of the trade. Since, DJ Apex has performed at various occasions such as Fashion Shows, Black Tie Corporate Events, Weddings, Sweet 16’s/Quinceanera’s, Countless Private Parties, Bars/Lounges and Nightclubs ranging from New York to Virginia.

Slick

Muraldy Gustave best known as DJ Slick, was born and raised in Haiti. He started DJing at 15 years old with a love of Caribbean music and a single laptop. Over time, Slick honed his skills, upgraded his equipment and developed a passion for making people dance. He quickly gained attention in Haiti which led to multiple bookings for various occasions and a gig with a local radio station. Although he had fell in love with Caribbean music he still desired to explore other music and culture.

At 19 years old Slick took a leap of faith to follow his dreams and moved from Haiti to the USA. Establishing himself as DJ in the States was a challenge, but he continued to push himself. DJ Slick started recording his own mixes, working on his scratching, developing as a producer and learning many new genres of music. Slick is young, fresh, and knows the tracks that keep people moving on the dance floor.

Slyde

Matt K, aka DJ Slyde, is no stranger to diversity. Hailing from the melting pot of Seattle, DJ Slyde found his infatuation with music at the age of 4. His first love of 90s hip-hop and R&B inspired him to take part of the revolutionary culture that helped bridged the inner city together. DJ Slyde began making cassette tape mixes of his favorite tracks in elementary school and eventually began dispersing mixtapes throughout high school to supply local parties with the hottest tracks. At the ripe age of 19, DJ Slyde moved to Japan to expand his horizons and found his calling as a DJ. A self taught DJ, DJ Slyde started off performing at small local bars. His popularity soared within a year and his parties became the premier spots for partygoers in the area.  He eventually snagged residencies at the hottest clubs in Tokyo and started to gain bookings for private events and weddings.

After moving back to the states in 2017, DJ Slyde took a brief hiatus from DJing to focus on his professional life. Realizing life without music as meaningless, DJ Slyde decided to fill the void by resurrecting his passion for DJing. DJ Slyde credits the city of Philadelphia as his motivation for further pursuing his DJ craft and is going at full speed to give his audiences the party they have been looking for.

Nicki T

For the past 16 years I’ve hosted events for Temple University, Ursinus College, Cabrini College, bars, clubs, countless weddings & private events. Philosofox

I started out with an iPod, an aux cord, and an air of musical superiority at Baptist High basement parties over a decade ago. My roommate and I had the most popular show on Messiah College radio, and the professionalism I learned there helped me make my hobby pay for itself. Diving into the Philly bar scene after some time in England and California, I decided that meant my own turntables, then CDJs.

Now I can rock a party with equipment as eclectic as the music, whether that means the vinyl my wife wishes wasn’t filling our basement, a remix I’m working on for a friend’s band, or my kids’ latest obsession (Lately it’s the Benny Benassi remix of Public Enemy’s “Bring the Noise”). There’s nothing I love more than blending old and new, popular and obscure, to find the perfect mix for any event!
